01

Positioning

Why we exist

Richup proved people will play board games with strangers on the internet. turnUp is built for a narrower, warmer case: getting your actual friends into a room when you can't all be in the same room.

Mission

Turn any group chat into a game night in under sixty seconds.

Loud, not corporate

We sound like the friend who suggests the game, not the app that hosts it.

Nostalgic, not retro

The games are old. The product never feels old. No skeuomorphic board textures.

Fast over fancy

Every screen exists to get a dice in someone's hand quicker.

Chat is the room

Voice and text aren't a feature bolted on — they're why anyone stays past round one.

02

Logo & mark

The turn icon

The mark is a single curved arrow closing a loop — a turn, a spinner, a token rounding the board. It works as a standalone app icon and as the dot that finishes the wordmark.

turnUp
Do
  • Keep 1x clearspace (the icon's own radius) on every side
  • Use the lime-on-violet primary mark whenever background allows
  • Let the mark sit alone as an app icon — no wordmark crammed in
Don't
  • Recolor the loop to anything outside the palette
  • Stretch, skew or add a drop shadow to the mark
  • Place the wordmark on busy photography without a solid plate

04

Typography

Round headlines, sharp scores

Fredoka carries the personality. Manrope carries the reading. Space Mono carries the numbers — room codes, timers, scoreboards.

Display — Fredoka 600

Roll the dice

Headlines, the wordmark, big in-game moments. Never set in body copy — it gets tiring past a few words.

Body / UI — Manrope 400–700

Manrope handles every interface label, chat message and settings screen. It stays legible at small sizes on mobile, where most games actually get played.

Utility — Space Mono

06

Voice & tone

Talk like a player, not a platform

Sentence case, active verbs, zero corporate filler. Errors explain what happened in plain words. Empty states invite the next move instead of apologizing.

Say this

"Your turn, champ."

Turn prompt — short, personal, no exclamation needed.

Not this

"It is now your turn to play!"

Reads like a system notice, not a friend.

Say this

"Connection dropped. Reconnect to keep your spot."

States what happened, then what to do.

Not this

"Error: WebSocket connection terminated unexpectedly."

Never surface system or network jargon to players.

07

In product

How it feels in the room

Dark Ink Indigo surfaces by default — it reads as a room at night, makes every token and chat bubble pop, and is easy on the eyes for long sessions.
